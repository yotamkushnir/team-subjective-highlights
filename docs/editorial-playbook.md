# Highlight reel editorial playbook (plain English)

Practical takeaways for video editing and social — derived from aggregate winner-channel vs loser-channel patterns (2–3 minute match highlights). Use as **directional** instinct, not a rigid formula; brand, opponent, and how rough the result was still override averages.

---

## When we **win**, the reel tends to…

- **Breathe more.** Fewer total clips, but **each moment stays on screen longer**, and the **whole video is a bit longer** (~4%). It feels less frantic, more “let this moment land.”
- **Lean into joy.** After **our** goals, we spend **a lot more time** on **celebrations** (especially in games where both teams scored). The story is: we scored, and we’re *feeling* it — linger on hugs, runs, crowd, bench.
- **Show our misses a bit less.** We include **fewer** “we almost scored” moments overall — the edit doesn’t need to prove we *could* have; the result already did.
- **Let the rival sweat (a little).** When they fluff a chance, we give **more** of **their** frustrated reactions — it reads as “they had their moments but couldn’t take them,” which flatters our win.

---

## When we **lose**, the reel tends to…

- **Cut faster and pack more in.** **More clips**, **shorter** average clip — snappier, more “here’s everything that happened,” less time to sit in any single beat.
- **Spend less time celebrating our goals** (again, where both teams scored). We still show we scored, but we **don’t milk** the happy bits as long — the mood of the day pulls the cut forward.
- **Show more of our near-goals.** **More** own-team **non-goal chances** — often reads as “we were in it,” “look how close it was,” or simply “we didn’t get the breaks.” It’s the classic “we fought / could’ve been different” layer.
- **Give the opponent a bit more non-goal spotlight.** **More replays** of their **non-goal** highlights (saves, close calls, etc.) — not necessarily more *chances* in the data, but **more editorial emphasis** on those moments. Visually, the opponent’s story gets a bit more air.

---

## One-line takeaway for the desk

**Wins** sell **emotion and clarity**: longer holds, bigger celebrations, fewer “almost” clips, a touch of rival pain. **Losses** sell **effort and narrative**: quicker pace, more “our chances” and “their moments” to frame the game, with celebrations trimmed so the piece doesn’t pretend it was a party.

---

## Caveat

Sample sizes in the underlying study are modest (*n* in the teens–30s for some metrics). Treat this as **directional** editorial instinct, not a proof — adjust for context every time.
